National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, Subcontract from University of Maryland
Dynamics of Coupled Ocean-Atmosphere Processes in the Indian Ocean
Arthur J. Miller, Ragu Murtugudde (UMd), and Markus Jochum (NCAR)
Award: $30,000
Duration: 2009
Project Summary.
The basic understanding of the influence of El Nino suggests a stronger subsidence over West Pacific and southeast Asia due to a modified Walker circulation over the Pacific and hence it results in a deficient monsoon. The 1987 El Nino and to a large extent, the 1982 El Nino resulted in a deficient Asian summer monsoon. However, in 1997, the very strong El Nino resulted in surplus rainfall over the Asian continent and a concomitant stronger monsoon circulation. The 2002 El Nino resulted in a drought over India and Asia. We plan to study how the Maddden-Julian Oscillations, preceding the El Nino events, modify the influence of the anomalous Walker circulation and anomalous local Hadley Circulation over the Indian Ocean. We will also try and understand the influence of El Ninos on the Asian summer monsoon better.
The project involves analyzing the output of a coupled ocean-atmosphere model of the Indian Ocean sector, which was run by Dr. Hyodae Seo (Hawaii). Graduate student Subramanian will execute statistical analysis of the output to attempt to identify the coupled and uncoupled variations that lead to these effects on the monsoon. Supplemental runs of the uncoupled atmosphere and uncoupled ocean, forced by the coupled model SST and surface flux fields, respectively, will aid in this analysis. Miller (SIO), Murtugudde (UMd), Jochum (NCAR) and Seo (Hawaii) will jointly lead the effort which will become a part of Subramanian's Ph.D. thesis.
