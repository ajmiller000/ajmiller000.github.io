Drew:
More than 30 years ago, 18-yr-old Jenny Rola. and 20-yr-old Artie Miller
left behind their lives in Missouri and Ohio
to seek adventure and beauty in the pine forests and mountain ranges
of Yellowstone National Park during the summer of 1978.

By chance, they found each other
in the glorious sunshine and glittering stars of the wilderness
and in their attending a performance of "South Pacific" in the Teton Theatre in Jackson Hole.
And a romance blossomed at the Old Faithful Lodge.

to the Golden Shores of the California Coast,
Jenny and Art have merged their lives and families.

and they find themselves compatible, supportive, positive, passionate,
and, above all, loving beings.

Drew:
Jenny and Art, you may now give your vows to each other.

Jenny:
As if time stood still,
you accepted me back into your life
with open arms and a warm open heart.
This second chance has allowed me to appreciate and recognize
the beautiful person you are and how lucky I am.

I take you Arthur Joseph Miller to be my husband
to love, honor, cherish and respect all the days of my life.
I look forward to every day with you and want to grow old together in love.
"The more I give, the more I have, for both are infinite."

Art:
From that spellbound jeep ride through Yellowstone and the Tetons,
to the electric enchantment of at last looking into your beautiful eyes once again.
From the fear of humiliation and rejection
to the courage of revelation and commitment.
From just my imagination running away with me,
to trading all my tomorrows for a single yesterday that can now last forever.

Jenny Rola', I've waited 30 years to say this.
I take you as my wife,
with all the love and understanding of my heart and spirit,
here now, for you, spoken freely and passionately
in front of our families and friends.
