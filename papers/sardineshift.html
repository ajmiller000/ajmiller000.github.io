T. R. Baumgartner, G. A. Auad and A. J. Miller, 2001:
Pacific Sardine Habitat Changes Induced by a Climate Shift
Fisheries Oceanography, to be submitted.
Abstract. The variability of Pacific sardine populations on interdecadal timescales is studied in the context habitat changes induced by changing physical oceanographic conditions associated with the 1976-77 climate regime shift. A primitive equation ocean model is forced with atmospheric fields that are meant to represent canonical regime changes which may also apply to past sardine population variations observed in sediment records. The model ocean produces a 40% net increase in sardine favorable habitat when the ocean climate is in the warm phase in the California Current region. Nutrient enrichment of the upper ocean may also play a role in establishing the favorable habitat.
